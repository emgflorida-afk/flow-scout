0DTE Iron Rules — Always Apply
01 — Wait for OR
Never enter the first 5 minutes. Let the opening range form (first 30 min). The OR high and low are your intraday bias anchors.
02 — Flip is Binary
Above the gamma flip = long bias only. Below = short bias only. No counter-trend trades in the first 2 hours unless the flip level is reclaimed.
03 — 50% Stop Always
0DTE options: stop = 50% of premium paid, set GTC the moment you fill. No exceptions. 0DTE decay is ruthless — a bad hold costs everything.
04 — Vol Zone = No Entry
The Vol Zone (−GEX) is a fuel cell, not support. Do NOT enter at the vol zone — enter after it breaks or bounces. It amplifies in both directions.
05 — First Target, Then Trail
Take 50% off at first GEX target. Move stop to entry on remainder. Never let a winner become a loser on a 0DTE. Time decay has no mercy after 2pm.
06 — Catalyst = Wider Levels
CPI, Fed, tariff news = implied move expands. GEX levels shift. On catalyst days, use the vol zone as your directional fuel signal, not a level to fade.

BULL PUT SPREAD — When to Use
Use when you believe a GEX support level will hold. You sell a put at or just below the support, buy a lower put as your hedge. IV being elevated (VIX above 20) means you collect more premium than normal. Best used when the Strat shows a Failed 2 Down at a major GEX floor. Your max profit is the credit received. Your max loss is the spread width minus credit.
BEAR CALL SPREAD — When to Use
Use when you believe price will stay below a GEX resistance level. You sell a call at or just above resistance, buy a higher call as your hedge. Works well when the Strat shows Failed 2 Up at the gamma flip or pin level. IV crush works in your favor — as vol compresses after a catalyst, your spread loses value faster. Max profit is the credit. Max loss is spread width minus credit.
WHY $5 WIDE FOR SPX RIGHT NOW
In a high-catalyst environment (tariffs, geopolitical, Fed), moves are fast and unpredictable. A $5 wide spread means your max loss is capped at $500 per contract (minus credit received). You stay in the game even if one trade goes wrong. Wider spreads ($25–$50) collect more premium but expose you to larger losses in a volatile tape. Start $5 wide until you have conviction on direction.
HIGH IV = SELL PREMIUM, NOT BUY
When VIX is above 20–25, buying naked options is expensive. IV will compress after the catalyst passes (IV crush) and destroy your premium even if you're right on direction. Spreads let you be a net seller of premium — you collect the inflated IV instead of paying it. This is the core advantage of spreads in environments exactly like the current market.

5. Strike Selection — The OTM/0DTE Edge
★
GEX Rating 5/5 — Go OTM / 0DTE
Maximum conviction. Like the VRT trade — +$561M at one strike, 5/5 rating. OTM options give max leverage on a high-probability move. This is where $1.15 → $3.50 in 2 min happens.
3-4
GEX Rating 3–4/5 — ATM or slightly OTM
Good edge but not maximum conviction. ATM reduces risk of the move not quite reaching your strike. Balance leverage vs probability.
1-2
GEX Rating 1–2/5 — ITM or skip
Weak GEX structure. If you trade at all, go ITM for delta and protection. Better to wait for a cleaner setup than force a low-conviction trade.
!
The key insight
The data determines your strike, not habit. Every move is unique. Let the GEX rating and distance to pin drive whether you go OTM or ITM — not emotion, not "I always buy ATM."
